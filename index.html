:: ABOUT ME ::

I am a third year engineering student currently enrolled in Conestoga College's ESE (Electronic Systems Engineering) bachelor program. I have a passion for learning new technologies and applying my skills and knowledge to contribute meaningfully to projects.

Kevin Hartwig


The Electronic Systems Engineering program at Conestoga boasts focus on a wide range of concepts, but has a hard focus on embedded hardware and embedded software principles, with courses dedicated to each. Computer science is also studied in detail in such courses as Data Structures and Agorithms, Data Communications and Networks, and Operating Systems.

I personally have a knack for programming and love tackling problems that can be addressed with code, particularily in C. This is the language that I have become most comfortable with throughout my years at Conestoga thus far. However, I am also familiar with C's younger brother, C++, and it's much more rarely used father, assembly language.

One of the major projects undertaken during this program has been the design of an embedded microcontroller board. Courses that focused on embedded hardware and embedded programming principles allowed me to write the low level software to drive the variety of peripherals that were included on the board.
